"Kuantha Firfir" is a popular Ethiopian dish made with injera, a traditional Ethiopian flatbread. It is a variation of the dish called "Kitcha Firfir."

Ingredients. Kuantha Firfir is typically enjoyed as a breakfast or brunch dish in Ethiopia. It's a hearty and flavorful dish that is often served alongside other Ethiopian dishes like scrambled eggs (known as "Kai Wot") and spicy lentil stew (known as "Misir Wot").

  • 4-6 pieces of injera (Ethiopian flatbread)
  • 1 cup berbere sauce (a spicy Ethiopian spice blend)
  • 1 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced clarified butter), or substitute with regular butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

Instructions or steps to do the above food

  1. Tear the injera into small pieces and set aside.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium heat and add the niter kibbeh or butter. Once melted, add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan and cook for another minute, stirring continuously.
  4. Add the berbere sauce to the pan and stir well to combine with the onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Gradually add water to the pan, starting with 1/2 cup and increasing as needed to create a thick but pourable sauce consistency. Stir well to ensure the sauce is evenly mixed.
  6. Add the torn injera to the pan and gently mix it with the sauce. The injera will absorb the flavors and soften. Cook for a few more minutes until the injera is heated through.
  7. Taste the dish and season with salt as desired.
  8. Serve the Kuantha Firfir hot, garnished with your choice of optional toppings such as chopped hard-boiled eggs, crumbled feta cheese, chopped tomatoes, and green onions.
