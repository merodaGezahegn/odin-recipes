Ethiopian kuantha firfir

Kuantha Firfir is typically enjoyed as a breakfast or brunch dish in Ethiopia. It's a hearty and flavorful dish that is often served alongside other Ethiopian dishes like scrambled eggs (known as "Kai Wot") and spicy lentil stew (known as "Misir Wot"). Ingredients.

  • 4-6 pieces of injera (Ethiopian flatbread)
  • 1 cup berbere sauce (a spicy Ethiopian spice blend)
  • 1 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced clarified butter), or substitute with regular butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

Instructions or steps to do the above food

  1. Tear the injera into small pieces and set aside.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium heat and add the niter kibbeh or butter..
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan and cook for another minute, stirring continuously.
  4. Add the berbere sauce to the pan and stir well to combine with the onions, garlic.
  5. Gradually add water to the pan, starting with 1/2 cup and increasing as needed to create a thick but pourable sauce consistency.
  6. Add the torn injera to the pan and gently mix it with the sauce.
  7. Taste the dish and season with salt as desired.
  8. Serve the Kuantha Firfir hot
