Ethiopian Doro wot Chicken

Ethiopian kitfo is a traditional dish that is popular in Ethiopian cuisine, particularly in the Gurage and Addis Ababa regions. It is made from minced raw beef, typically taken from the leaner cuts such as sirloin or tenderloin. Kitfo is often considered a delicacy and is commonly served on special occasions or as part of a festive meal. Ingredients

  • 2 pounds (approx. 1 kg) chicken pieces (preferably bone-in, such as drumsticks or thighs)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, lemon juice, and berbere spice. Mix well, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated.
  2. Sauté the onions, garlic, and ginger: Add the finely chopped onions to the pot and sauté until they become soft and translucent.
  3. Add the spices: Add the berbere spice, paprika, cardamom, coriander, cumin, fenugreek, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg to the pot.
  4. Add the chicken: Place the marinated chicken pieces into the pot, along with any excess marinade.
  5. Incorporate the liquid: Pour in the chicken broth or water and add the tomato paste.
  6. Simmer the stew: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the stew simmer gently for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours.
  7. Garnish and serve: Once the stew is cooked, remove it from the heat.
  8. Serving suggestion: Ethiopian Doro Wot is traditionally served with injera, a sourdough flatbread.
